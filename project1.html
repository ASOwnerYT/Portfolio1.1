Project Overview

Problem: Australia's Post Office Website Can Be Improved.

Proposed Solution: After Researching the Problem with Usability Tests, Redesign the Australia Post Office Website Using UX Best Practices.

Empathize

Government websites are notorious for having bad design. Users are often frustrated trying to find the information they are searching for. For this project, I chose to redesign the australia post office navigation and homepage.

Proto-Persona

Ken owns a small printing business in australia, where he resides. As Ken's business has grown, his need for using the postal service has grown. The current website feels convoluted and hard to navigate.

Usability Testing

To better understand where users struggle using the australia post office website, which parts aren't functional, and where the navigation and homepage can be improved.

Heuristic Evaluation & Accessibility

The biggest issue with the homepage was its accessibility and performance. According to the lighthouse report, the homepage scored 50% in performance, and from my own research using the 6 main colors, the homepage had a 60% failure rate for accessibility. You can see my color audit Here.

Define

Card Sort

Users were frustrated that there was multiple ways to carry out an action within the megamenu. I sorted all of the L1, and L2 options, deleted duplicates, and tried to group them more intuitively.

Updated Site Map

Many items were nested farther than the L2. The 'Tools' section was deleted and converted into a CTA bar that was visible above the fold.

Ideate

My efforts were concentrated on the main navigation for desktop and mobile views.

Prototype

The Main Navigation went through multiple iterations before I was happy with the design. It was important for it to be responsive. I originally wanted to incorporate a carousel but ultimately discarded it because it was too distracting from the main content. The original homepage felt very text heavy and I wanted to make it feel more intuitive. I got rid of the horizontal cards and created a simple grid of cards.

Test

Since the original goal was to improve the navigation, it made the most sense to conduct A/B testing. Due to time constraints, only two A/B tests were completed. 50% of users utilized the "Tools" sections in the original navigation and 50% did not. Some key insights were to continue iterating on the L1 titles as well as the CTA bar copy.
