Australia Post Office

    • Project Overview

    Problem: Australia's Post Office Website Can Be Improved.

    Proposed Solution: After researching the problem with usability tests, redesign the Australia Post Office homepage using UX best practices.

    Empathize

    • Government websites are notorious for having poor user design
    • Users are often frustrated trying to find the information they are searching for
    • This case study showcases my redesign of The Australia Post Office main navigation and homepage
    Proto-Persona
    • Ken owns a small printing business in Australia, where he resides
    • As Ken's business has grown, his need for using the postal service has grown
    • The current website feels convoluted and hard to navigate
    Usability Testing
    Goal: To better understand where users struggle using the Australia Post Office website, which parts aren't functional, and where the site can be improved.
    Heuristic Evaluation & Accessibility
    • The biggest issue with the homepage was its accessibility and performance
    • On The Lighthouse report, the homepage scored 50% in performance
    • From my own research using the 6 main colors, the homepage had a 60% failure rate for color contrast accessibility. You can see my color audit Here.

    Define

    Card Sort
    1. Users were frustrated that there were multiple ways to carry out an action within the megamenu.
    2. I sorted all of the L1, and L2 options, deleted duplicates, and tried to group items more intuitively. (In hindsight, it would have been much more beneficial to engage users at this point of the research.)
    Updated Site Map

    Many items were nested farther than the L2. The 'Tools' section was deleted and converted into a CTA bar that was visible above the fold.

    Ideate

    My efforts were concentrated on the main navigation for desktop and mobile views. It went through multiple iterations before I was happy with the design. It was important for it to be responsive.

    Prototype

    I originally wanted to incorporate a carousel but ultimately discarded it because it was too distracting from the main content. The original homepage felt very text heavy and I wanted to make it feel more intuitive. I got rid of the horizontal cards and created a simple grid of cards.

    Test

    The original goal was to improve the navigation so it made the most sense to conduct A/B testing against the original desktop navigation bar and my redesigned desktop navigation bar.

    • Due to time constraints, only two A/B tests were completed.
    • 50% of users utilized the "Tools" sections in the original navigation and 50% did not.
    • Some key insights were to continue iterating on the L1 titles as well as the CTA bar copy.
    • You can see my A/B Testing plan here.

    Solution & Next Steps

    Based on the results from the A/B testing, I would

    • combine "Send Post" and "Collect Post" into "Manage Mail."
    • I'd also conduct another card sort with those categories, this time modified with user-input.
    • I'd add more padding in the CTA bar and conduct more usability tests.

    I really enjoyed this redesign project and think I made a lot of improvements in general usability and accessibility. Play around with my desktop working prototype here!
